title: "What is DataHawk"
description: "Understand what DataHawk collects, structures, and delivers."
---

DataHawk connects your Amazon and Walmart accounts, structures the data, and delivers it through dashboards and managed databases so you can analyze performance without building your own pipeline first.

## What you get

- Structured data from Amazon and Walmart accounts, products, keywords, and categories
- Modeled datasets with curated KPIs such as sales estimates and keyword search volume
- Managed database delivery through destinations such as Snowflake and BigQuery
- Ready-to-use dashboards, alerting, and AI-assisted apps

## What questions it helps answer

DataHawk is built to help sellers, vendors, advertising managers, finance teams, and analysts answer questions like:

- Why did revenue change this week?
- Which products drive profit or margin loss?
- Which campaigns have the weakest efficiency?
- Which products are at risk of going out of stock?
- How is your brand performing against competitors in tracked markets?

## Main outputs

DataHawk gives you two main output paths:

1. Pre-built dashboards for fast analysis in tools such as Power BI and Looker Studio.
2. A structured database for custom BI, reporting, analytics, and data science workflows.

## Supported data areas

- Seller Central sales, traffic, fees, inventory, and profit inputs
- Vendor Central revenue, margins, traffic, demand, and inventory
- Amazon and Walmart advertising performance
- Product, keyword, and category tracking
- Market and competitive intelligence

<Tip>
  Active setup time is usually around 30 minutes, then DataHawk needs time to process and load your data.
</Tip>
