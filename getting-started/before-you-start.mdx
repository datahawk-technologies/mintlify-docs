---
title: "Before you start"
description: "Prepare your accounts, access, and optional inputs before setup."
---

Before you connect your data, make sure you have the right access and tooling. This avoids setup delays later.

## Sign-in

You should receive an invitation email from DataHawk when your account is ready. Use that link to sign in. You do not need to create a separate account manually.

## Accounts you may need

Connect only the accounts that apply to your business.

### Amazon

- **Amazon Seller Central** if you sell as a third-party seller
- **Amazon Vendor Central** if you sell to Amazon as a first-party supplier
- **Amazon Advertising** if you want Sponsored Products, Sponsored Brands, Sponsored Display, or DSP data

### Walmart

- **Walmart Marketplace** if you sell on Walmart.com
- **Walmart Connect** if you want Walmart advertising data

## Optional inputs

### Cost of goods sold

If you want P&L metrics such as gross profit, operating profit, or net profit, you need to upload your cost of goods sold data. Without COGS, revenue data still works, but profit metrics stay incomplete.

### BI tooling

If you plan to use Power BI templates, confirm you have:

- Power BI Desktop to open and customize templates locally, or
- Power BI Pro or Premium to publish and share dashboards

## Timing

After you connect a source, DataHawk needs time to retrieve and process historical data. Plan for roughly 24 hours before you expect reports to be fully populated.

## Checklist

- Invitation email received
- Admin access to each Amazon account you plan to connect
- Access to Walmart accounts you plan to connect
- COGS file prepared if you want profit reporting
- Power BI access confirmed if you plan to use Power BI templates
