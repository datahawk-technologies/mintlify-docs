This guide takes you from sign-in to your first report.

## Step 1: Sign in

Open the invitation email from DataHawk and use the sign-in link. If you do not see the email, check spam first, then contact your DataHawk account manager.

## Step 2: Understand the app

After sign-in, get familiar with these areas:

- **Workspace**: isolated environment for a brand, client, or business unit
- **Sources**: where you connect Amazon and Walmart data
- **Apps**: built-in tools such as Sherlock, Alerts, AI Copywriter, and Insights
- **Destinations**: where you configure databases and downstream integrations

## Step 3: Connect your sources

Go to **Sources** and connect the accounts relevant to your business:

- Amazon Seller Central
- Amazon Vendor Central
- Amazon Advertising
- Walmart Marketplace
- Walmart Connect

Only connect what applies to you. Once connected, DataHawk starts the initial sync.

## Step 4: Upload COGS if needed

If you want profit reporting, upload your cost of goods sold data after setup. This is optional for revenue and traffic reporting, but required for complete P&L metrics.

## Step 5: Add a destination

If you want to work outside the app, add a destination such as Snowflake or BigQuery. DataHawk creates a managed database and gives you credentials you can use in BI tools and spreadsheets.

## Step 6: Open dashboards

When the initial sync finishes, start with:

- Power BI templates for packaged reporting
- Looker Studio for a lighter overview
- DataHawk apps for alerts, anomalies, and investigation
