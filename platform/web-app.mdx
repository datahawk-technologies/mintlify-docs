The DataHawk web app is the control plane for your workspaces, sources, destinations, and apps.

## Core concepts

- **Workspace**: isolated environment for one brand, client, or segment
- **Sources**: connected Amazon and Walmart accounts, products, keywords, and categories
- **Destinations**: managed databases and downstream integrations
- **Apps**: tools built on top of your connected data

## Account and workspace management

From **Settings**, you can:

- Update your name, email, and password
- Download your personal data
- Create new workspaces
- Review workspace capability usage
- Invite members and manage roles

### Roles

- **Admin**: can manage members and billing
- **Member**: can work with data but cannot manage workspace administration

## Billing

If your account uses Stripe, the billing portal lets you:

- Review invoice history
- Update payment methods
- Update billing information
- Pay pending invoices

## API access

The API Reference area includes:

- Your API key
- Your workspace ID
- Links to the API documentation used to manage tracked entities and integrations

## Built-in apps

### AI Copywriter

AI Copywriter generates optimized Amazon listing content based on your product data, keyword opportunities, and marketplace best practices.

### Alerts

Alerts send daily email notifications when tracked products change in ways you care about, including price, Buy Box, ratings, listing details, stock, and more.

### Insights

Insights surfaces findings, anomalies, and best-practice guidance across ads, products, sales, SEO, and market data. It supports assignment and status tracking so teams can collaborate around issues.

### Sherlock

Sherlock is DataHawk’s AI-powered diagnostic engine for product-level performance shifts. It is designed to detect major changes, explain likely drivers, and recommend the next actions.

<Info>
  Sherlock availability depends on your account setup and product rollout stage.
</Info>
