DataHawk organizes data into schemas and tables so you can work at different levels of abstraction, from raw ingestion to curated reports.

## Main schema families

### `ADVERTISING`

Advertising performance by account, campaign, keyword, product, and platform-specific reporting layers.

### `FINANCE`

Seller financial and order data, including orders, events, account metrics, product metrics, and profit and loss tables.

### `MARKET`

Market intelligence data such as browse-node estimates, category-level ranks, and product sales estimates.

### `PRODUCT`

Tracked product metadata, listing quality, competitor data, image links, history, and AI-generated product content.

### `RAW_INVENTORY`

FBA inventory snapshots, ledger events, and replenishment recommendations.

### `REFERENTIAL`

Reference entities such as marketplaces, tracked products, tracked keywords, accounts, tags, and exchange rates.

### `REPORTS`

Curated and enriched reporting tables that combine data across schemas for analytics use cases.

### `SELLING_PARTNER`

Seller and vendor operational tables, including traffic, returns, forecasts, fees, and inventory-related data.

### `SEO`

Keyword volume plus organic and sponsored rank tracking.

### `USAGE`

Snowflake usage and destination activity data where applicable.

## Common questions

### Where do I find sessions and page views?

Use Seller traffic tables in the `SELLING_PARTNER` area, especially the traffic tables broken down by ASIN, parent ASIN, or SKU.

### Where do I find category leaders?

Use market and reporting tables that combine category ranks, estimates, and brand fields.

### Where do I find keyword rank data?

Use the `SEO` schema for ranking tables or `REPORTS` for enriched rank datasets.

### Why are there raw and preview labels?

- **Raw** means the dataset stays close to the original source payload.
- **Preview** means the dataset may still evolve in structure or coverage.
