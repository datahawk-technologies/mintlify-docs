Sources are how data enters DataHawk. You can connect private account data and public tracking data.

## Private account sources

### Amazon Seller Central

Seller Central gives you access to sales, orders, units sold, returns, refunds, reimbursements, marketing costs, traffic, and inventory. DataHawk supports Seller Central accounts across North America, Europe, and the Far East, and stores daily refreshed data with up to two years of historical depth for some datasets.

### Amazon Vendor Central

Vendor Central adds vendor revenue, Amazon revenue, units sold, returns, product margins, traffic, inventory, and demand forecasts. DataHawk retrieves up to three years of historical data and refreshes it daily, subject to Amazon API delays.

### Amazon Advertising

Advertising sources centralize campaign, ad group, keyword, targeting, and product-level ad performance. DataHawk stores more history than the standard Amazon Ads API window and makes the data available for dashboards and database access.

## Public tracking sources

### Product tracking

Track Amazon and Walmart product pages to monitor:

- price and Buy Box state
- ratings and review counts
- listing content and media
- category and sales rank information
- availability and variation details

### Keyword tracking

Track Amazon and Walmart keywords daily to monitor:

- organic rank
- page placement
- sponsored placement
- basic metadata for ranked products

### Category tracking

Amazon category tracking monitors the top 100 products in a category and captures simplified product and rank metrics for market analysis.

## Tags and organization

Use tags to organize tracked products and keywords. Tags help with filtering, segmentation, and downstream reporting.

## Operational notes

- Most tracked-source data refreshes daily
- Initial sync can take up to 24 hours
- Marketplace support varies by source type and API availability
- Some recent data may be delayed because source platforms do not finalize it immediately
